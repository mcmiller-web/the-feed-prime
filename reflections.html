What I hope the internet will be like in the future?

In the future, I hope the Internet will have more authentic spaces where people share genuine and "handcrafted" work with the world, given that we'll have hundreds of technological tools to use by then. I'd want fewer people to be controlled by these dominating social platforms, such as Instagram and X, and instead have platforms that are created by people, for people, with an interconnected network that doesn't force everyone to compete for "airtime" on the Internet.

I'd also want the youth on the Internet to have more restricted access to content and online material that is vulgar and too mature for them. This would mainly involve better regulations on what content is directed at whom and the use of content walls, at least to a degree that would minimize the exposure better than the systems we have now.

Life Online in CONTRAST to Life IRL?

When I think about the contrasts of life online vs. life in real life, I think about the authenticity of it all. Though we have people who do express their authentically real selves and everyday lives (even more so now), we still have to acknowledge or understand that at the end of the day, there’s a side of them that we won’t actually be able to see simply because we’re not really connecting with them on an organic level.

In real life, as you navigate the world and interact with people, your authentic connection of face-to-face impressions is what leads you to growing a network of people and influences that will pour into how to present yourself and view the world. And if you’re lucky, some of those connections will grow so deep that you do actually get to see the whole person and persona of an individual—and that’s part of what makes life worth living and existing in reality for. As hard as it is (even for myself), stay away from screens and touch grass, y’all!
